1 Understanding the Requirements

The STI2D final oral exam lasts 20 minutes and consists of 3 parts:

  • 5 minutes of standing presentation (without notes)
  • 10 minutes of discussion with the jury
  • 5 minutes discussing your future study plans

Tip: Your presentation should demonstrate how your STI2D project addresses a technical problem related to your specialty subjects.

2 Choosing Your Topic

Your topic should:

  • Be related to your STI2D specialties
  • Address a concrete technical problem
  • Show your personal reflection
  • Present innovative solutions

Possible Topic Examples:

ITEC (Innovation Technology and Eco-Design): "How to optimize the design of a mechanical system to reduce its environmental impact while maintaining performance?"

EE (Energy and Environment): "To what extent can a home automation solution contribute to energy management in a household?"

SIN (Digital Information Systems): "How to secure data in an IoT system while ensuring its accessibility?"

AC (Architecture and Construction): "What architectural solutions allow for both thermal comfort and reduced carbon footprint in buildings?"

3 Structuring Your Presentation

Structure your presentation in 3 clear parts:

  1. Introduction: Present the problem and its context
  2. Development: Explain your approach and solutions
  3. Conclusion: Answer the problem and discuss future perspectives

Typical Structure:

  • Hook (statistic, quote, question)
  • Project context
  • Clear problem statement
  • Outline of your presentation
  • Presentation of your approach
  • Technical solutions considered
  • Critical analysis of solutions
  • Results obtained
  • Answer to the problem
  • Critical summary
  • Improvement perspectives
  • Connection to your future plans

4 Preparing Visual Aids

You can use visual aids (not evaluated) to illustrate your presentation:

  • Maximum 3 slides (PowerPoint, Prezi, etc.)
  • Focus on diagrams, prototype photos, graphs
  • Avoid long texts

Warning: Visual aids are only support - your oral presentation should be understandable without them.

5 Practicing Your Presentation

To succeed:

  • Practice in front of a mirror, then with friends/family
  • Time yourself to respect the 5-minute limit
  • Work on your posture and voice
  • Anticipate possible questions

    During the 10-minute discussion:

    • Listen carefully to the questions
    • Take a few seconds to structure your answer
    • Admit if you don't know an answer
    • Stay calm and professional

    Tip: The jury wants to assess your ability to discuss, not trap you. An answer like "I haven't studied this aspect but I think that..." shows your reasoning skills.

    • Explain how your STI2D project contributes to it
